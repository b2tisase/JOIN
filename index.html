TBF PROJECT (TBFP)

The purpose of the foundation is: Education, social activism and development in Ethiopia.

The TBF PROJECT offers a unique way to get involved with projects at an intimate level. It is a program in which you, along with a group of like-minded individuals, take responsibility for a specific project.

TBF PROJECT volunteers will gain a first-hand understanding of issues related to education and witness a project grow over time. With a secure source of long-term support, the project is able to establish firm roots and move towards self-sufficiency.

Each TBF PROJECT MEMBER guarantees financial support of $200 per year towards their own project. Once committed, the TBF PROJECT volunteer works on making the project self-reliant. As the teams are small, accountability is self-contained. Progress reports are posted on the web regularly, thus spreading awareness and enabling new volunteers to join the effort. TBF PROJECT MEMBERS are not confined to group meetings or their geographic location. They bridge the distances through commitment from the very beginning.

There was a perfect fit between the model and the ground realities. Within three years, two TBF project members emerged to set TBF Projects on a stable course for the long haul.

Initially, we proposed three TBF PROJECT MEMBERS (i.e., three involved owners) as ideal. Early on in the process, however, we identified three as being an artificial limit and a disadvantage, especially in handling large projects that needed a larger investment than $600 (three members contributing $200 each). So, we decided to call our organization The Bitew Foundation (TBF), where the number of volunteers is a variable. It made meaningful and poetic sense. This is how TBF PROJECT was born.

Qualification of a TBF Project Member

Active involvement: Each member must have a vision for where the project is heading. He/she must feel connected and involved with the project’s future and take ownership for its long-term success.

Visit the project: Each member must visit the project site in Ethiopia at least once within two years of joining the project. If a personal site-visit is not possible, a site-visit by a TBF-ETHIOPIA member may be arranged. It is important for project members to become closely involved with children and volunteers at the project site.

Financial support: The member guarantees financial support of at least $200 per year. These funds must consistently be delivered to the TBF Project co-coordinator two months before the money is sent to the project. The member also works with the co-coordinator to raise any further funds needed from the TBF branch or through other fundraisers. Members must remember that if funds are not delivered in a timely manner, the stress put on the volunteers in Ethiopia distracts from the real work with the children. Timely delivery of funds must be considered imperative for the project’s success. Students who cannot guarantee a $200 contribution at present may become TBF volunteers.

TBF PROJECT GUIDELINES

TBF Project Selection Guidelines

[The following are the TBF Project selection guidelines]

TBF’s mission is to identify projects aimed at providing basic education and development to underprivileged children in Ethiopia. Our emphasis is on projects that implement innovative approaches to education and basic development.

We ensure that the projects are funded and monitored constantly. We preferentially fund projects in the state (country) with the lower literacy rate, and that is located away from an urbanized region.

How We Work

After evaluating a project proposal, we decide if we will fund it for at least ¾ years. The newly selected projects are assigned a coordinator who is responsible for monitoring progress and ensuring that regular funding payments are sent. We work with other branches to share project funding efforts.

Selection process

After evaluating the proposal, TBF arranges a site visit, which is usually conducted by a member or someone else we rely upon, such as a friend or relative. When the paper “plan” matches the actual site, the project may be approved for funding.
